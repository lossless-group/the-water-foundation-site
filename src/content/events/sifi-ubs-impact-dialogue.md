A Lunchtime Dialogue for Foundations and Philanthropic Investors in Hamburg.

On 25 March 2026, in Hamburg, the SDG Impact Finance Initiative (SIFI) and the UBS Optimus Foundation convened a lunchtime dialogue with 30–40 representatives from leading German foundations and philanthropic investors.

The focus was clear: how can philanthropic capital be deployed more strategically and with greater leverage for sustainable development?

The conversation quickly moved beyond traditional grantmaking. Participants explored how catalytic capital can be used to unlock private investment, drawing on practical instruments such as guarantees, subordinated capital, and outcomes-based financing. The role of platforms also came into focus, not as intermediaries, but as enabling infrastructure to align public, philanthropic, and private actors.

One figure anchored the discussion: European foundations distribute around EUR 76 billion in grants annually, while holding over EUR 500 billion in capital.

A significant share of that capital remains untapped. The question is no longer whether foundations can do more, but how to translate that potential into scalable impact.

Hamburg reinforced a broader shift already underway: from isolated interventions to more structured, system-level approaches to mobilising capital.
